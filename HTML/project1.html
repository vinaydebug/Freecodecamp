Rajma Recipe (North Indian Kidney Bean Curry)

Rajma is a popular North Indian dish made with red kidney beans cooked in a rich, spiced tomato-based gravy. It is a comforting and nutritious meal, often served with steamed rice, known as “rajma chawal,” a favorite in many Indian households.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup rajma (kidney beans)
  • 3 cups water (for soaking)
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 medium onions (finely chopped)
  • 2 medium tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies (slit)
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)
  • 2–3 cups water (for cooking)

Instructions

  1. Wash and soak rajma overnight in enough water.
  2. Drain the soaked rajma and pressure cook with fresh water and a little salt for 4–5 whistles or until soft.
  3. Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  4. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  5. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for 1–2 minutes.
  6. Add tomato puree and cook until oil separates from the mixture.
  7. Add turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well.
  8. Add cooked rajma along with its water. Stir and let it simmer for 15–20 minutes.
  9. Mash a few beans to thicken the gravy.
  10. Add garam masala and mix well.
  11. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  12. Serve hot with rice or roti.
